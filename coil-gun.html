Coil Gun

Context

Assisting a fellow grade 12 student, I contributed to a physics project focused on determining the velocity of a slug. I helped to design and organize the experiment, carefully configuring the necessary tools. To achieve this, I engineered two distinct chronograph systems: the first employing a laser, and the second utilizing an IR sensor. Once data collection concluded, my friend proceeded to independently compose the project report.

Process

For a more comprehensive understanding of the process, you can refer to the detailed description in the student's report below. On this project page, I'll be focusing on sharing the tasks I undertook and the skills I acquired throughout the project.

Skills I gained

Through this project, I refined critical thinking in designing physics experiment tools, creatively integrating a light-sensitive resistor with a laser for a precise chronograph, and successfully implementing IR sensors. I also gained valuable experience in managing capacitors, while also acquiring new skills in soldering and the operation of DC power generators.

Sad Fact

Unfortunately, I don't have a video of the second experiment setup, but I do have this photo that I used while creating this page. In this setup, I used a unique method to measure something. I directed a laser at a light-sensitive resistor. When a slug was released and blocked the laser, the resistor detected a change in light, and its resistance increased. By measuring the time it took for this resistance to change, I could calculate the speed of the slug.
